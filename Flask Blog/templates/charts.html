Market Capitalization in the PSE

The following charts and insights below will help us dig deeper into the valuation of the PSE through the financial concept known as Market Capitalization.

As of the second quarter of 2020, the Philippine Stock Exchange (PSE) is valued at a market cap of 13 Trillion Philippine Pesos (263B USD).

Market Capitalization, or Market Cap, can be described as the value of a company in the stock market according to what investors are willing to pay for it. This perceived value is calculated by multiplying the total number of shares with the company's current share price. This value can indicate the growth prospects of the company, its inclination to risk, and the reward it can bring forth to the investor with that risk.




An Overview of the Philippine Stock Exchange's Total Market Cap



This overview gives us an idea of the publicly traded companies within the Philippine Stock Exchange that are greatly valued by investors. A large majority of these companies are local business giants and international players. The chart also indicates the range of market cap values starting from 88 million pesos to 1.18 trillion pesos.

The most valuable company based on market capitalization according to our data is Manulife Financial Corporation, a Canada based multinational insurance company. It is followed by SM Investments Corporation, and Sun Life Financial Corporation, another Canada based multinational insurance provider. These three companies already make up one-fourth of the PSE's total market cap value. This gives you an idea of the scale of these companies.

The Financial Sector



With a market cap of 3.5 trillion PHP, the Financial Sector ranks at the top of the other PSE sectors. The multinational insurance companies, Manulife and Sun Life, are the most valuable companies in this sector, with their values more than half of the local leading banking company, BDO Unibank.




The Holding Firm Sector



Holding Firms are companies that own shares of other companies. They do not deliver their own services or products, but rather invest in the services and products of other companies in various industries.

And with a market cap of 3.2 trillion PHP, SM Investments leads the pack at 1.1 trillion PHP, with the Ayala Corporation and JG Summit Holdings, trailing at only half the value of SM Investments. Still, Ayala Corporation is valued at 492 billion PHP, and JG Summit Holdings at 444 billion PHP.




The Industrial Sector



The Industrial Sector is ranked at the third of the most valuable list in terms of market capitalization amongst all the other sectors. At a market cap value of 2.2 billion PHP, the Industrial sector is dominated by the fast moving consumer goods and power distribution companies. San Miguel Food and Beverage (404B) tops this sector, followed by the Manila Electric Company (300B), Universal Robina Corporation (285B), and Aboitiz Power (200B).




The Mining and Oil Sector



The Mining and Oil Sector in the Philippines is valued at 178 billion pesos. Semirara Power and Mining leads the whole sector at 53 billion pesos, with the Atok Big-Wedge Company and the Nickel Asia Corporation trailing at half the value at 25.9 billion pesos and 24.6 billion pesos, respectively.

The Service Sector



The telecommunications sector with Globe at 284 billion pesos, and PLDT at 270 billion pesos, lead the Service sector. Razon's port operations company ICTSI follows at 202 billion pesos. Villar's funeral park developer Golden Bria Holdings at 181 billion pesos is the only other company to cross the sector's halfway mark value.




The Small, Medium, and Emerging Board Sector



The Small, Medium, and Emerging Board (SME) sector is the PSE's growth sector for small, and medium entreprises looking to utilize the benefits of publicly listing themselves in the stock market for their expansion plans. There are only a handful number of companies in the PSE's SME sector, which is currently valued at 36 billion pesos.

Notable companies include Sia's recent listing of MerryMart valued at 26 billion pesos, and Philab Holdings, a life sciences equipment supplier turned holdings company, is valued at 5.8 billion pesos.




The Exchange Traded Fund (ETF) Sector



Exchange Traded Funds are investment funds traded on stock exchanges, much like stocks. ETFs hold assets such as stocks, commodities, or bonds and generally operate with an arbitrage mechanism designed to keep it trading close to its net asset value, although deviations can occasionally occur.

The PSE currently only lists one ETF, the First Metro Philippine Equity Exchange Traded Fund (FMETF). The FMETF is managed by the First Metro Asset Management, Inc. The fund is currently valued at 1.58 billion pesos.
